[33;1m[1;3mNone[0m[32;1m[1;3mHere are five of the most influential recent papers on Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting in large language models.  They include the original CoT work, key improvements (self-consistency, least-to-most), and methods for automatically constructing and refining CoT demonstrations.

1. “Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models”  
   • Jason Wei, Xuezhi Wang, Dale Schuurmans, Maarten Bosma, Brian Ichter, Fei Xia, Ed Chi, Quoc Le, Denny Zhou  
   • arXiv:2201.11903 (January 2022; updated v6 Jan 2023)  
   • Introduces CoT prompting: by prepending a few examples showing step-by-step reasoning, very large LMs (e.g., PaLM-540B) dramatically improve on arithmetic, commonsense, and symbolic reasoning benchmarks (e.g., GSM8K).  
   • Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903  

2. “Self-Consistency Improves Chain-of-Thought Reasoning in Language Models”  
   • Xuezhi Wang, Jason Wei, Dale Schuurmans, Quoc Le, Ed Chi, Sharan Narang, Aakanksha Chowdhery, Denny Zhou  
   • arXiv:2203.11171 (ICLR 2023)  
   • Replaces greedy decoding of a single CoT with sampling many reasoning chains and “voting” on the most consistent answer.  Achieves large gains (+10–18 points) across arithmetic and commonsense benchmarks (GSM8K, SVAMP, AQuA).  
   • Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.11171  

3. “Least-to-Most Prompting Enables Complex Reasoning in Large Language Models”  
   • Denny Zhou, Nathanaël Schärli, Le Hou, Jason Wei, Nathan Scales, Xuezhi Wang, Dale Schuurmans, Claire Cui, Olivier Bousquet, Quoc Le, Ed Chi  
   • arXiv:2205.10625 (ICLR 2023)  
   • Decomposes hard problems into a sequence of simpler sub-questions: prompt the model first on an easy subtask, feed its answer back in to solve the next subtask, and so on.  On SCAN compositional generalization and math benchmarks it far outperforms vanilla CoT.  
   • Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.10625  

4. “Synthetic Prompting: Generating Chain-of-Thought Demonstrations for Large Language Models”  
   • Zhihong Shao, Yeyun Gong, Yelong Shen, Minlie Huang, Nan Duan, Weizhu Chen  
   • arXiv:2302.00618 (2023)  
   • Uses an existing CoT-prompted LLM to synthesize new high-quality chain-of-thought examples automatically, alternating between generating questions from reasoning chains and refining reasoning chains for questions.  Outperforms manual few-shot CoT across numerical, symbolic, and algorithmic reasoning tasks.  
   • Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.00618  

5. “Reprompting: Automated Chain-of-Thought Prompt Inference Through Gibbs Sampling”  
   • Weijia Xu, Andrzej Banburski-Fahey, Nebojsa Jojic  
   • arXiv:2305.09993 (ICML 2024)  
   • Introduces an iterative Gibbs-sampling approach to automatically discover effective CoT prompt templates (“recipes”) by treating them as latent variables and sampling new prompts conditioned on training examples.  Outperforms human-written CoT and other prompt-optimization methods on 20 reasoning tasks.  
   • Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.09993  

These five papers chart the development of Chain-of-Thought prompting—from the basic idea of exposing step-by-step rationales, to improvements in decoding (self-consistency), structured decomposition (least-to-most), and methods for automatically generating or optimizing CoT prompts.[0m
